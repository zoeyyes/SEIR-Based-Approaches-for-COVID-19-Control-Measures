It has been found that group-specific measures may not require a significant sacrifice in terms of overall deaths, even under aggressive assumptions with 2.5 times the beta. Our findings suggest the total death rate is roughly the same between group-specific measures and uniform social measures. Additionally, the ICU capacity was not exceeded for high-risk groups, highlighting the feasibility of group-specific measures in protecting vulnerable populations. Moreover, the actual ICU utilization for the low-risk group may be even better than anticipated given there would be available ICU beds left over for high-risk individuals. This underscores the potential benefits of group-specific measures in improving the epidemic situation and maintaining the economic functions of society.
